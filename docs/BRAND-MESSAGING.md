## Objection Handling

### "Can't we just use Community Edition?"

**Response:**
> "Community Edition has 21 slots optimized for single apps. When you have a monorepo with 10+ packages, those 21 slots can't capture workspace structure, build orchestration, or versioning strategy. That's why we built the 33-slot Enterprise Edition."

### "Why not just use better documentation?"

**Response:**
> "Documentation goes stale. FAF Enterprise is bi-synced with your codebase. When your Turborepo structure changes, your .faf updates automatically. Documentation can't do that."

### "Isn't this just for large enterprises?"

**Response:**
> "If you have a monorepo with 10+ packages, you're enterprise-scale—even if you're a 5-person team. Enterprise Edition is about codebase complexity, not company size."

### "How is this different from Nx/Turborepo docs?"

**Response:**
> "Nx and Turborepo document the tools. FAF Enterprise documents YOUR monorepo—your packages, your architecture, your team's decisions. It's project DNA, not framework docs."
